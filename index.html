Voting Frequency Positively Related to Education and Income Levels

In general, there appears to be a positive relationship between voter frequency and both income and education. We can track trends in this relationship by following the paths of each voter frequency category.






2020 Elections see Large Spike in Absentee Ballots Submitted

There is a large spike in percent of votes casted as absentee ballots during the 2020 election compared to others. The reasoning behind this dramatic difference in absentee voting is that 2020 marked the beginning of COVID-19, a global pandemic that effectively brought physical social interactions to a halt. During the 2020 elections there was a lot of emphasis on voting by mail to uphold social distancing.






Voter Turnout in Southwest Virginia Generally Lower than Rest of State

Based on the map, we can see that in general Southwest Virginia has the lowest voter turnout in the state, with some localities having turnouts of 33-39%. On the other hand, areas north of Richmond appear to have higher voter turnouts, with turnouts around or above 50%.
